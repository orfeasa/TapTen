Tap Ten Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 30, 2026

Who this policy applies to

This policy covers the Tap Ten iPhone app, the public website at playtapten.com, and support or beta-signup flows linked from those surfaces.

Data stored on your device

Tap Ten is designed as a local, single-device party game. Gameplay data such as saved settings, bundled content, local custom packs, and in-app progress stays on your device unless you choose to use an external contact or reporting feature.

Tap Ten does not require account creation to play.

Question reports and support requests

If you use Flag question in the app, Tap Ten may prepare and store a report containing question metadata, your selected reason, your optional note, app version information, and a timestamp so the issue can be reviewed.

If a report delivery endpoint is enabled for your build and available, that report may be sent to Tap Ten’s content-review service. If delivery is unavailable, the report may remain on your device until it can be retried or until the app is removed.

If you contact support through the App Store listing support contact or another external support channel, we will receive the information you include so we can respond and handle the request.

Beta signup and third-party services

The Tap Ten website links to a Google Form for beta-interest signup. If you open or submit that form, the information you provide is collected by Google Forms and handled under Google’s own policies, not solely by this website. See Google’s Privacy Policy.

TestFlight beta distribution and App Store purchases, where applicable, are also handled through Apple services and are subject to Apple’s policies and platform terms. See Apple’s Privacy Policy.

What Tap Ten does not do

Tap Ten is not built around public profiles, advertising networks, or social feeds. The public website is a static site and does not intentionally include third-party analytics scripts or ad trackers.

Retention and deletion

Local data stored by the app generally remains on your device until you delete it, reset it in the app, or remove the app from your device.

If Tap Ten receives a support email or server-side question report, that information may be kept for as long as reasonably needed to respond, review content issues, maintain records, and resolve follow-up requests.

To request deletion of support correspondence or server-side report data that you sent to us directly, use the Tap Ten App Store listing support contact.

Contact

For privacy questions or support requests, use the Tap Ten App Store listing support contact.
